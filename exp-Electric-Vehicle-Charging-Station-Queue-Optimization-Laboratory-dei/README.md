### About the Experiment

## Overview

This experiment introduces the basic concepts of queueing theory, EV charging station management, and smart energy distribution. Students learn how different factors such as vehicle arrival rate, number of charging bays, charging time, and queue capacity affect the overall performance of an EV charging station.

The virtual laboratory simulates a multi-bay EV charging station connected to a smart grid. Students can change various input parameters and observe how they influence queue length, waiting time, charger utilization, and grid power consumption in real time.

The experiment also demonstrates two queueing models:

* M/M/c – Infinite queue, where vehicles can always wait.
* M/M/c/K – Finite queue, where vehicles leave if the waiting area is full.

Students can also study real-world situations such as increased vehicle arrivals, limited charging capacity, queue overflow, and grid power limitations. The simulator compares practical results with theoretical queueing calculations, helping students understand the relationship between mathematical models and real-world EV charging operations.

This experiment provides practical knowledge of queue optimization, charger utilization, load management, and smart grid operation, which are essential concepts in modern electric vehicle infrastructure and smart city engineering.
