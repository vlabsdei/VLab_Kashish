### About the Experiment

This experiment introduces students to the principles of intelligent microgrid operation and renewable energy integration within a modern smart power grid. Students investigate how variables such as solar irradiance, wind speed, ambient temperature, biomass fuel feed rates, and consumer demands influence power distribution efficiency, storage utilization, and overall grid stability.

The experiment provides an interactive simulation of a signalized smart microgrid network, including solar photovoltaic fields, wind turbine arrays, a dispatchable biomass plant, lithium battery storage, a hydrogen electrolyzer/fuel cell system, and the utility grid connection. Students can modify generation, storage, and consumer demand parameters and observe their impact on power routing, battery state of charge, hydrogen levels, grid imports/exports, and system telemetry in real time.

The experiment also demonstrates real-world energy management challenges such as renewable intermittency, high peak-demand grid congestion, grid outages, and off-grid standalone operations. By analyzing these scenarios, students gain an understanding of how intelligent Energy Management System (EMS) dispatch strategies—including Peak Grid Shaving, Maximum Self-Consumption, and Battery Backup Support—can reduce utility grid strain, optimize clean energy consumption, and support the development of sustainable smart city energy infrastructure.

This virtual laboratory helps learners explore the relationship between volatile renewable energy generation and dispatch control strategies while providing practical insights into modern power systems engineering, green energy storage technologies, and intelligent electrical utility management.
