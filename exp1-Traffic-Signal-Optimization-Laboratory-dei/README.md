### About the Experiment

This experiment introduces students to the principles of intelligent traffic management and traffic signal optimization at a four-way road intersection. Students investigate how factors such as vehicle arrival rate, signal timing, traffic density, and queue length influence traffic flow efficiency and overall intersection performance.

The experiment provides an interactive simulation of a signalized intersection, including traffic lights, multiple traffic lanes, and vehicles approaching from the North, South, East, and West directions. Students can modify traffic parameters and observe their impact on vehicle movement, waiting time, congestion levels, and traffic throughput in real time.

The experiment also demonstrates real-world traffic management challenges such as peak-hour congestion, uneven traffic distribution, and queue buildup at intersections. By analyzing these scenarios, students gain an understanding of how adaptive traffic signal control systems can reduce delays, improve road utilization, and support the development of smart city transportation infrastructure.

This virtual laboratory helps learners explore the relationship between traffic demand and signal control strategies while providing practical insights into modern urban traffic engineering and intelligent transportation systems.
