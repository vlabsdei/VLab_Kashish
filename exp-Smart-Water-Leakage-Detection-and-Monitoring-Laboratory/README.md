### About the Experiment

This experiment introduces students to the principles of smart water distribution systems and pipeline leak detection using Internet of Things (IoT) technology. Students investigate how parameters such as water flow rate, pipeline pressure, pump speed, and leak severity influence the performance and safety of a water distribution network.

The experiment provides an interactive simulation of a water pipeline equipped with flow sensors, a pressure sensor, a soil moisture sensor, an IoT controller, and an automated solenoid valve. Students can create different leakage conditions, adjust pump speed and safety thresholds, and observe their effects on water flow, pressure, water loss, and system status in real time.

The experiment also demonstrates real-world challenges faced in water distribution networks, including minor leaks, major leaks, pipe bursts, and water wastage. By analyzing sensor readings and comparing different operating conditions, students gain an understanding of how IoT-based monitoring and automatic shutoff systems can quickly detect leaks, isolate damaged pipeline sections, and minimize water loss.

This virtual laboratory helps learners explore the relationship between flow rate, pressure variation, leak detection, and automated control strategies, while providing practical insights into smart water infrastructure, intelligent monitoring systems, and modern water resource management.
