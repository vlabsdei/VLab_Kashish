Laboratory Lab Manual

Aim of the Experiment

To design, monitor, and configure an IoT-enabled smart water leakage detection and pipeline monitoring system. Specifically, we will study:

  • The Mass Balance Method (flow difference) for finding pipe leakage.
  • The Pressure Drop Method to analyze localized pressure changes due to leaks.
  • IoT automation loop rules for solenoid shutoff valves.

Theory

Water loss in distribution networks is a critical global challenge. Traditional leakage detection is manual, slow, and expensive. Smart infrastructure utilizes sensors connected to a centralized gateway to monitor pipeline telemetry continuously.

1. Flow Mismatch (Mass Balance)

By comparing the flow rates at two points along a line (Inlet Flow \(Q_{in}\) at sensor FS-01 and Outlet Flow \(Q_{out}\) at sensor FS-02), a leak rate \(Q_{leak}\) is calculated:

\[Q_{leak} = Q_{in} - Q_{out}\]

If \(Q_{leak}\) exceeds a sensor noise tolerance threshold (\(\Delta_{limit}\)), a leak is flagged.

2. Pressure Gradient Method

When water exits through a leakage hole, pressure at that zone drops significantly. The flow velocity increases near the leak, leading to localized friction losses. We monitor pressure \(P\) via sensor PT-01. A sharp decline below threshold \(P_{limit}\) indicates pipe failure.

3. Solenoid Valve Control (IoT loop)

Solenoid valves are electromechanical valves. An microcontroller run script checks sensor telemetry: 

IF (Qin - Qout > Limit) OR (Pressure < Plimit)
    SET SolenoidValve = CLOSED
    TRIGGER AudioBuzzer = ACTIVE

Procedure

  1. Set Base Flow Rate: Power up the main water pump using the slider (e.g. 60%). Verify that Flow Sensor 1 and Flow Sensor 2 show equal readings, and the Line Pressure is steady.
  2. Simulate Leakage: Choose a leak severity (Minor, Major, or Pipe Burst) using the control panel, or click directly on one of the Zones (1, 2, or 3) on the pipe.
  3. Observe telemetry changes:
    • Watch how the flow rates diverge (Outlet Flow drops while Inlet Flow might slightly rise or drop).
    • Observe the dramatic pressure drop in the system.
    • Note the water spraying animation and soil moisture grids lighting up below the leak zone.
  4. Test IoT Automation: Enable the "IoT Auto-Shutoff Rule". Induce a leak and notice that the solenoid valve closes instantly, terminating water loss and isolating the pipeline.
  5. Record Trials: Perform tests at different pump speeds (e.g., 30%, 60%, 90%) and leakage severities. Click "Record State" to add observations to the lab table. Export the data as CSV for your lab report!
