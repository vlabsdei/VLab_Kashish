### Procedure

1. Open the Smart Grid Energy Distribution Laboratory simulation.

2. Observe the system components, including:

   * Solar Power Plant
   * Wind Power Plant
   * Smart Grid Controller
   * Distribution Network
   * Residential Load
   * Commercial Load
   * Industrial Load

3. Adjust the Solar Generation slider to set the desired solar power output.

4. Adjust the Wind Generation slider to set the desired wind power output.

5. Adjust the Residential Load slider to define residential energy demand.

6. Adjust the Commercial Load slider to define commercial energy demand.

7. Adjust the Industrial Load slider to define industrial energy demand.

8. Click the Start Simulation button to begin the smart grid operation.

9. Observe the energy flow from renewable energy sources to the Smart Grid Controller and Distribution Network.

10. Monitor the following performance parameters displayed in the simulation:

    * Total Generated Power
    * Total Demand
    * Power Surplus / Deficit
    * Grid Efficiency
    * Renewable Utilization
    * Grid Condition

11. Analyze the effect of varying generation and load values on overall grid performance.

12. Observe the grid condition under different operating scenarios:

    * Power Surplus (Generated Power > Total Demand)
    * Balanced Condition (Generated Power = Total Demand)
    * Power Deficit (Generated Power < Total Demand)

13. Compare the calculated results with the theoretical formulas provided in the experiment.

14. Repeat the simulation with different combinations of generation and demand values to study smart grid behavior under various conditions.

15. Click the Reset button to restore default values and perform additional experiments.
