### About the Experiment

This experiment introduces students to the concepts of smart grid technology and intelligent energy distribution systems used in modern smart cities. Students investigate how renewable energy sources such as solar power and wind power can be integrated into a smart grid to meet varying consumer demands efficiently.

The experiment provides an interactive simulation of a Smart Grid Energy Distribution System consisting of Solar Power Plant, Wind Power Plant, Smart Grid Controller, Distribution Network, and different categories of consumers including Residential, Commercial, and Industrial loads. Students can modify generation and demand parameters and observe their impact on grid performance.

The simulation demonstrates how generated power is managed and distributed through a smart grid infrastructure. Students can analyze key performance indicators such as Total Generated Power, Total Demand, Power Surplus or Deficit, Grid Efficiency, Renewable Utilization, and Grid Condition.

The experiment helps learners understand renewable energy integration, intelligent power management, demand-response behavior, and efficient energy distribution mechanisms that form the foundation of modern smart city energy systems.
